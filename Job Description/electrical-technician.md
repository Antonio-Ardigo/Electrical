# Job Description — Electrical Technician (Site Survey & Data Capture)

**Engagement:** Electrical **Single Point of Failure (SPOF) Assessment** of 21 water /
desalination plants across the Central & Eastern sector (Riyadh, Madinah, Qassim,
Eastern Region), over a **4-month** campaign.

> **Note:** The core team is **1 Project Manager + 2 Electrical Engineers**. This
> technician profile is an **optional field-support role** to strengthen the
> non-intrusive site surveys and the asset / document inventory; include only if the
> scope and budget allow.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Reports to** | Electrical Engineer / Project Manager (Electrical) |
| **Engagement** | Site-survey phases of the 4-month campaign |
| **Location** | Plant sites (Central & Eastern) + office |
| **Languages** | **Arabic & English** |

## Role summary

Provide hands-on field support to the assessment engineers during the **non-intrusive
site surveys**: capture as-found electrical data, support the asset / document inventory
and the verification of single-line diagrams, all under the engineers' direction and
within the host QHSE and permit-to-work rules.

## Key responsibilities

- **Site survey support** — accompany the engineers on **non-intrusive** field surveys and
  help locate and identify MV / LV equipment against the drawings.
- **Asset & document inventory** — record equipment nameplate data (ratings, make / model,
  tags) for switchgear, transformers, LV switchboards / PCC, MCCs, VFDs, power-factor
  correction, generators, transfer switches, UPS, batteries / chargers and earthing —
  populating the **document-inventory** form.
- **Single-line diagram verification support** — assist in **red-lining single-line
  diagrams** against the as-found installation and note discrepancies for the engineers.
- **Data capture** — photographs, panel / label readings, switch / breaker status and
  configuration, and accessible meter readings; organise the survey data per plant.
- **Condition observations** — note visible condition, housekeeping, thermographic
  hot-spots (where permitted) and obvious reliability concerns (e.g. duty / standby on a
  shared board) for engineer follow-up.
- **HSE & data** — strictly follow host **permit-to-work**, lock-out / tag-out and QHSE
  (ISO 45001) rules; **non-intrusive only**; comply with the NDA and the applicable
  national cybersecurity data-handling requirements (no retention or disclosure of client
  data).

## Minimum qualifications

- Electrical **technician / trade qualification** (diploma or equivalent) and a valid
  electrician's licence where required.
- 3+ years in industrial electrical installations; **LV competence**, **MV awareness**.
- Comfortable **reading single-line and wiring diagrams** and identifying equipment by tag.
- Familiar with electrical **survey / test instruments** (multimeter, clamp meter,
  insulation / earth tester, IR thermography) for non-intrusive use.
- **Arabic & English** working proficiency; valid driving licence (multi-site travel).

## Skills & competencies

- Accurate field data capture and record-keeping; methodical and safety-first.
- Awareness of the relevant IEC / IEEE / NFPA 70E requirements at a technician level.
- Team player able to work to the engineers' instructions across multiple plant visits.

## Key deliverables — the forms

- Per-plant **survey data packs** (nameplate inventory, photos, configuration notes) that
  populate the **document-inventory** and **site-visit-report** forms.
- Red-line mark-up support for the single-line-diagram verification.
- Clean, organised data handed to the engineers for the assessment forms.

*Optional role; inclusion, scope and rate are subject to agreement.*
