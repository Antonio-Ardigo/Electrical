Worked Example

Worked example — Apparent power and transformer sizing

Given an indicative total active power demand of 2 MW at a plant power factor of 0.85 lagging, calculate the required transformer apparent power and select a standard size.

Step 1 — Apparent power required:

S = P / pf = 2000 kW / 0.85 ≈ 2353 kVA

Step 2 — Allow ~10 % contingency:

S_design = 2353 × 1.10 ≈ 2588 kVA

Step 3 — Split across two transformers (N+1 split-bus configuration):

S_each = 2588 / 2 ≈ 1294 kVA → select 2 × 1600 kVA (next standard size)

Result: 2 × 1600 kVA, Dyn11, 11 kV / 415 V transformers (tags TX-1 and TX-2). All values are indicative.

Pre-Assessment Checklist

The list below demonstrates the persistent checklist feature. Tick items as you complete them; state is saved in browser local storage keyed to this section's id.

  • Obtain current single-line diagram (SLD) and equipment schedule from the client.
  • Confirm supply voltage and frequency with the utility (target: 11 kV ± 6 %, 50 Hz).
  • Verify transformer nameplate data matches design specification (kVA, voltage ratio, vector group).
  • Check protective relay settings and coordination study is current.
  • Confirm diesel generator auto-transfer time and load shedding sequence.
