The Assignment

Where you are in the workflow: before anything else, understand what the campaign is and what you are being asked to deliver.

The SPOF Assessment Campaign

You are one of two Electrical Engineers on a four-month campaign to deliver an Electrical Single Point of Failure (SPOF) Assessment across 21 groundwater desalination plants in the Central & Eastern sector — 19 in Riyadh Province and 2 in the Eastern Province (no plants in Madinah or Qassim), 16 sites, ~1.66 M m³/day total. The core team is:

Role Count Primary responsibility
Project Manager (Electrical) 1 Programme, quality, HSE, client interface; reviews all deliverables before submission.
Electrical Engineer 2 Per-plant SPOF assessment, plant calculation packages, concept modifications, assessment forms. That is you.
Electrical Technician Optional Non-intrusive field surveys and asset inventory support only — not part of the core team.

What you are asked to do per plant

For each of the 21 plants you are expected to work autonomously through the following:

  • Desk study: review the existing single-line diagrams, equipment schedules and prior studies.
  • Plant calculation package: produce load-flow (verifying bus voltages and cable loadings under normal and N-1 conditions), short-circuit (prospective fault current at each bus), protection coordination (relay grading), and earthing & lightning assessments with power-system software. Arc-flash per IEEE 1584 (incident energy at each working position) and RAM/RBD — Reliability, Availability, Maintainability / Reliability Block Diagram availability modelling (Part 3) — are advantageous.
  • SPOF assessment: inventory and classify electrical single points of failure; assess criticality using FMECA — Failure Mode, Effects and Criticality Analysis per IEC 60812 (Part 3) — which ranks each failure mode by Risk Priority Number (RPN = Severity × Occurrence × Detection, max 125 on the 1–5 scales used here).
  • SLD verification: red-line the single-line diagrams against the as-found plant during site visits.
  • Concept modifications: for each recommended change, prepare a marked-up SLD, equipment outline specification, scheme description, sequence of operation and high-level bill of materials. Concept level only — not detailed design, procurement or construction.
  • Assessment forms: complete the four per-plant deliverable forms (see Section — The Four Deliverable Forms).
Note — indicative figures: All ratings, capacities and figures in this manual are indicative and tentative. They illustrate method and typical practice. Confirm every value against the plant's actual load list, short-circuit study, utility connection agreement and governing specifications before acting on them.

Site logistics — Central & Eastern sector

The 21 plants sit at 16 sites — 19 in Riyadh Province (clustered around Riyadh, with outliers from Al-Aflaj in the south to Al-Artawiyah in the north) and 2 in the Eastern Province (Al-Hani and Hafar Al-Batin), ~1.66 M m³/day total. The map combines a national overview with a Riyadh-province zoom. City-level positions are indicative; marker size ~ capacity.

Key takeaway: You are an autonomous assessment engineer. Your job is to identify electrical single points of failure, quantify their risk, and recommend practicable mitigations — plant by plant, across 21 sites in four months.

The Four Deliverable Forms

Where you are in the workflow: understand the exact deliverables you must produce for every plant before you step on site.

For each of the 21 plants, the primary deliverables are four completed assessment forms plus the plant calculation package and any concept modification packages. The forms are described below. Blank templates are in Appendix F — Blank Forms.

Form What it contains When completed Who owns it
1. Document Inventory List of all electrical documents obtained or requested: single-line diagrams (revision, date, verified), equipment schedules, protection setting records, prior studies, maintenance records, drawings. Records what was available, what was missing, and what was verified on site. Started in desk study; updated on site visit; finalised after site. Lead Engineer (you) — PM reviews.
2. FMECA Form Failure Mode, Effects and Criticality Analysis (IEC 60812). One row per electrical item of significance: failure mode, local effect, system effect, existing controls, severity (S), occurrence (O), detectability (D), RPN or criticality number, and recommended action. Identifies and ranks the SPOFs. Populated during analysis phase after desk study and site visit; updated iteratively. Lead Engineer — PM quality-gates before submission.
3. Recommendations Register Ranked list of recommended modifications or improvements. One row per recommendation: reference number, description, priority (high/medium/low), estimated scope, applicable standard, link to FMECA row, and status (open / in progress / closed). The basis for the concept modification packages. Drafted after FMECA is complete; updated as concept modifications are developed. Lead Engineer — PM approves priority ranking before issue.
4. Site-Visit Report Factual record of the site visit: date, attendees, areas accessed, equipment observed, as-found conditions (nameplate data, breaker/switch status, visible condition concerns, any thermographic hot-spots if permitted), discrepancies against the SLD, and photographs index. Non-intrusive only — see the warning below. Completed on the day of the site visit; reviewed by PM before issue. Lead Engineer (with optional technician support for data capture).
Tip — form discipline: Open all four forms as soon as a plant is assigned. Fill the Document Inventory and Site-Visit Report header during desk study so the template is ready on site. Do not wait until after the visit to create the form.
Tip — traceability: Every row in the FMECA form should carry a unique reference that you can cite in the Recommendations Register. The PM uses this linkage when quality-checking your work.
Form & row-ID legend. SF-01 = Document Inventory; SF-02 = FMECA Worksheet; SF-03 = Recommendations Register; SF-04 = Site-Visit Report. Within each form, rows carry the following prefixes: DOC-xx (SF-01 document rows) | FM-xx (SF-02 FMECA rows) | REC-xx (SF-03 recommendation rows) | OBS-xx (SF-04 observation rows) | PH-xx (SF-04 photolog rows) | ACT-xx (SF-04 action rows). These prefixes are consistent across the blank forms in Appendix E and the worked Jubail example in Part 5.
Key takeaway: Four forms, completed in sequence — Document Inventory, FMECA, Recommendations Register, Site-Visit Report — are the non-negotiable deliverables for every plant. Start them early; update them iteratively.

Assessment Workflow at a Glance

Where you are in the workflow: map the full sequence so you can plan your time plant by plant.

Stage What you do Form(s) updated
1 — Pre-site desk study Request and review all available documents: SLDs (confirm revision / date), equipment schedules, prior calculations, protection setting records. Identify missing documents to chase on site. Prepare calculation model shell. Mark up a working copy of the SLD with questions. Brief the optional technician if deployed. Document Inventory (columns: document received, revision, status)
2 — Site visit (non-intrusive) Verify SLD against as-found installation; red-line discrepancies. Capture nameplate data (ratings, make/model, serial numbers) for all main equipment. Record breaker/switch configuration (normally open / closed). Note visible condition, housekeeping and reliability concerns. Photograph (where permitted) and index photos. Collect any documents not previously received. Strictly observe host permit-to-work and QHSE rules. Site-Visit Report (as-found data);
Document Inventory (documents obtained on site)
3 — Analysis (office) Update the calculation model with as-found data. Run load-flow, short-circuit, protection coordination and earthing/lightning studies. Identify and classify SPOFs from the verified SLD. Populate FMECA rows: failure mode, effects, controls, criticality (S × O × D). Rank items by criticality / risk. FMECA form (all columns)
4 — Recommendations For each high-criticality FMECA item, develop a concept modification: marked-up SLD, equipment outline specification, scheme description / sequence of operation, and high-level bill of materials. Re-run affected calculations on the modified arrangement. Populate the Recommendations Register with priority, scope and FMECA cross-reference. Recommendations Register (all columns)
5 — Report & issue PM reviews and quality-gates all four forms and the calculation package. Finalise the Site-Visit Report. Assemble the per-plant deliverable pack (forms + calculation package + concept modification packages + red-lined SLDs). Submit to PM for consolidation into the campaign report. All four forms finalised; calculation package issued.
Tip — iterate freely: The workflow above is a logical sequence, not a strict one-pass process. You will often loop back: a site finding may invalidate a desk-study assumption and require you to update the FMECA and re-run a calculation. Return to earlier stages whenever new information warrants it. The PM expects updated forms up until the submission milestone.
Key takeaway: Five stages — desk study, site visit, analysis, recommendations, report. The forms are living documents throughout; complete and finalise them in order.

How to use the manual on a real assignment

  1. First time through: read Parts 0 and 1 in full to internalise the workflow and the tag scheme. Skim Part 2 to confirm you are comfortable with the calculation methods.
  2. Starting a new plant: open Part 5 — Desk Study Procedure and follow the checklist. Open the four blank forms from Appendix F and fill the header information.
  3. On site: use the Visual Inspection Checklist and the Drawing Verification Checklist in Part 5. The SPOF symbol library in Part 3 helps you spot failure points in real switchrooms.
  4. During analysis: use Part 2 for calculations and Part 3 for the FMECA method. Cross-reference to the Formula Card and MTBF Reference Data as needed.
  5. New to this type of assignment? Start with the first-assignment walk-through in Part 5 — it takes you step-by-step through one complete plant cycle.
Tip — sidebar navigation: Use the sidebar (left panel) to jump between sections. Checkboxes in checklist sections persist across sessions in the same browser — use them to track progress per plant.
On-screen vs field use. Checkbox tick-state is stored in the browser's localStorage — it persists only in the same browser on the same device, and only while on-screen at a desk. For field use, print the relevant checklist section (use the Print button or Ctrl+P) and tick on paper. Transfer your paper marks back to the on-screen form in the office.
Key takeaway: Parts 0 and 1 are the foundation — read them first. Parts 2–4 are your reference library. Part 5 is your task guide on every assignment. Use the first-assignment walk-through in Part 5 if you are new to this type of work.

The "indicative figures" rule

Every numerical value in this manual — voltages, currents, kVA ratings, cable sizes, fault levels, reliability figures — is indicative and tentative. The values are sized to a representative ~2 MW industrial plant to illustrate calculation method and typical equipment selection. They are not a substitute for:

  • the plant's actual load list and demand study,
  • a site-specific short-circuit and load-flow study,
  • the utility connection agreement and network data,
  • the governing project specification and local regulations.

Always confirm figures against actual plant data before acting on them.

Warning — NON-INTRUSIVE surveys only. All site surveys under this campaign are non-intrusive. You must not open energised panels, operate switches or breakers, perform live electrical tests, or take measurements that require direct contact with live conductors. Visual inspection, nameplate reading, photograph capture and accessible meter readings are permitted. Strictly observe the host permit-to-work procedure, lock-out/tag-out rules and QHSE requirements (ISO 9001 / 14001 / 45001) at every plant. The NDA and applicable national cybersecurity data-handling requirements apply to all data collected on site.
Key takeaway: IEC basis, 11 kV / 400–415 V / 50 Hz, the tag scheme above, all figures indicative — and every site survey is non-intrusive. These four rules underpin everything in this manual.
